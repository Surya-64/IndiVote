Voting 101: Your Journey

A simple 5-step guide to exercising your democratic right in India.

1

Check Eligibility

Must be an Indian citizen, 18+ years old, and resident in your constituency.

2

Register to Vote

Apply via Form 6 on the Voter Portal. You'll need age and address proof.

3

Find Your Name

Verify your name on the electoral roll and locate your polling station.

4

Visit Polling Booth

Go to your booth on election day with a valid identity proof (EPIC/Aadhaar).

5

Cast Your Vote

Verify your details, get your finger inked, and press the button on the EVM.

Indian Democracy

Types of Elections in India

India conducts elections at multiple levels of government. Understand the structure of India's democratic electoral system.

🏛️
01

Lok Sabha Elections

The general election to elect Members of Parliament (MPs) to the lower house of India's Parliament. Held every 5 years.

  • 🗺️ 543 constituencies across India
  • 📅 Last held: April–May 2024
  • 👤 PM emerges from majority party/coalition
  • 📋 Conducted by Election Commission of India
Learn More →
🏩
02

Rajya Sabha Elections

Elections for the upper house of Parliament. Members are elected by State Legislative Assembly members, not directly by voters.

  • 💺 245 total seats (233 elected + 12 nominated)
  • 🔄 1/3rd seats renewed every 2 years
  • ⏳ 6-year term for each member
  • 🗳️ Indirect election by MLAs
Learn More →
🏢
03

State Assembly Elections

Elections to the Vidhan Sabha (State Legislative Assembly). Determines which party forms the state government and who becomes Chief Minister.

  • 🗺️ 28 States + 3 UTs with legislature
  • 📅 Every 5 years per state
  • 👤 CM emerges from majority party
  • 🗓️ 2026: Assam, Kerala, TN, WB, Puducherry
Learn More →
🌾
04

Panchayat Elections

Local body elections for rural governance. Elects representatives to Gram Panchayats, Panchayat Samitis, and Zila Parishads across villages.

  • 🏘️ 3-tier system: Village, Block, District
  • 📅 Every 5 years, varies by state
  • 🧑‍🤝‍🧑 Includes reserved seats for SC/ST/Women
  • 📋 Conducted by State Election Commission
Learn More →
🏙️
05

Municipal Corporation Elections

Elections to urban local bodies for city governance — Municipal Corporations, Municipalities, and Town Panchayats. Elects ward councillors and mayors.

  • 🏙️ Covers cities & towns across India
  • 📅 Every 5 years, varies by state
  • 🧑‍💼 Elects Mayor & Ward Councillors
  • 📋 Conducted by State Election Commission
Learn More →
⭐
06

Presidential Elections

The election of India's President and Vice-President. Not a direct public vote — elected by an Electoral College of elected MPs and MLAs.

  • 🏛️ Electoral College: MPs + MLAs
  • 📅 Every 5 years (next: 2027)
  • ⚖️ Proportional representation & single transferable vote
  • 📋 Conducted by ECI
Learn More →
🔁
07

By-Elections (उपचुनाव)

Held to fill a vacant seat in Parliament or State Assembly caused by death, resignation, disqualification, or court order before the term ends.

  • 🔔 Called within 6 months of vacancy
  • 📍 Only for the specific constituency
  • 🗓️ Can occur any time of year
  • 📋 Schedule set by ECI on case-by-case basis
Learn More →

FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

Any Indian citizen who is 18 years of age or older on the qualifying date (January 1 of the year of electoral roll revision) and is ordinarily resident of the constituency is eligible to vote. They must be registered on the electoral roll.

You can use your Voter ID (EPIC), Aadhaar Card, PAN Card, Driving License, Passport, Bank/Post Office Passbook with photo, or any government-issued photo ID. If your name appears on the electoral roll, you are entitled to vote.

Visit voters.eci.gov.in and fill out Form 6 online. You'll need to provide proof of age and address. You can also visit your local ERO (Electoral Registration Officer) office. The process typically takes 2–4 weeks.

EVM (Electronic Voting Machine) is a standalone device not connected to any network, making it immune to hacking. It has a real-time clock and logging system. VVPAT (Voter Verifiable Paper Audit Trail) slips allow voters to verify their vote. EVMs are subject to rigorous First Level Checking (FLC) before every election.

The Model Code of Conduct (MCC) is a set of guidelines issued by the ECI to political parties and candidates to regulate conduct during elections. It covers campaigns, speeches, rallies, government announcements, and polling day activities. It comes into effect when election dates are announced.
